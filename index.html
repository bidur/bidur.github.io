About

A concise academic and professional profile bringing together education, research, software engineering, and social impact.

I am a Nepali academic and technology professional with expertise in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, Data Science, GIS, Remote Sensing, WebGIS, and software development. I have served in major academic roles including Assistant Professor, Program Coordinator, and Graduate/Under Graduate Supervisor, while also leading industry-oriented and consulting work in software development, data science and analytics and digital systems.

My research and applied work span tourism monitoring, social media analytics, nighttime light remote sensing, land use analysis, mobility analysis, geospatial big data, and other software solutions. I am particularly interested in playing with data (data analysis) and building AI- and ICT-driven systems that address real societal needs.

I try to combine academic depth with practical implementation experience, using tools such as Python, R, Java, QGIS, Google Earth Engine, Tableau, JupyterLab, and modern machine learning workflows.

Vision / Career Goal

“To develop AI- and ICT-driven solutions that promote sustainable tourism and smart city development in Nepal, while contributing to broader social and economic transformation across multiple sectors. This includes building and deploying intelligent, scalable applications not only for tourism and agriculture but also for areas such as education, healthcare, environmental management, and public services to enhance efficiency, accessibility, and sustainability.

As a Nepali professional, I am committed to leveraging AI and ICT for social good by promoting digital inclusion through training, awareness programs, and teaching initiatives. I aim to empower individuals, communities, and institutions with the knowledge and skills needed to adopt and utilize emerging technologies effectively. By fostering innovation and responsible use of AI, I seek to support local economies, improve livelihoods, and contribute to Nepal’s long-term sustainable development.”
